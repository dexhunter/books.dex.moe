The books is available online freely.

Questions from Naval’s Almanack

  1. Q: According to Naval, what is the best way to build wealth?
💡 Click to see answer
Own equity in a business.
  1. Q: What does Naval mean when he says, “Productize Yourself”?
💡 Click to see answer
Convert your unique skills into scalable income sources.
  1. Q: Why does Naval emphasize “permissionless leverage” as a key to success?
💡 Click to see answer
It allows individuals to create wealth without needing approval from others.
  1. Q: Which of the following is not an example of leverage Naval promotes?
💡 Click to see answer
Leverage on a crypto exchange.

Explanation: Naval promotes code, media, and owning a business with network effects as forms of leverage.
  1. Q: Why does Naval say that renting out your time is not a path to wealth?
💡 Click to see answer
Your earning potential is capped by the number of hours you can work.
  1. Q: Naval argues that wealth is fundamentally different from money. What is wealth?
💡 Click to see answer
Valuable assets that earn while you sleep.
  1. Q: What does Naval mean by “Play long-term games with long-term people”?
💡 Click to see answer
Build relationships and work with people who think in decades, not months.
  1. Q: How did giving away The Almanack of Naval Ravikant for free impact its commercial success?
💡 Click to see answer
It increased demand for the physical book.
  1. Q: Naval discourages playing “status games.” Why?
💡 Click to see answer
Status games are zero-sum, whereas wealth creation is positive-sum.
  1. Q: What is one of Naval’s most important contributions to changing how people think about wealth?
💡 Click to see answer
Helping people see that wealth creation is not about luck.
